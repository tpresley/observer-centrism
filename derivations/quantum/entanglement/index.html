Entanglement from Relational Invariants

Statement

Theorem. Relational invariants between observers (Relational Invariants) map to entangled quantum states in the tensor product Hilbert space derived from coherence conservation (Born Rule). The coherence measure of the relational invariant equals the von Neumann entanglement entropy of the reduced state. The no-cloning theorem and entanglement monogamy follow as direct consequences of coherence conservation (Axiom 1) and coherence subadditivity.

Step 3: No-Cloning from Coherence Conservation

Theorem 3.1 (No-cloning theorem). It is impossible to construct a physical process that copies an arbitrary unknown quantum state. That is, there exists no unitary operator UUU acting on H⊗Hanc\mathcal{H} \otimes \mathcal{H}_{\text{anc}}H⊗Hanc​ such that:

U(∣ψ⟩⊗∣0⟩)=∣ψ⟩⊗∣ψ⟩U(|\psi\rangle \otimes |0\rangle) = |\psi\rangle \otimes |\psi\rangleU(∣ψ⟩⊗∣0⟩)=∣ψ⟩⊗∣ψ⟩

for all ∣ψ⟩∈H|\psi\rangle \in \mathcal{H}∣ψ⟩∈H.

Proof. The proof follows from coherence conservation (Axiom 1) applied to relational invariants.

Step 1 (Coherence accounting). The initial state ∣ψ⟩⊗∣0⟩|\psi\rangle \otimes |0\rangle∣ψ⟩⊗∣0⟩ has coherence C(∣ψ⟩)+C(∣0⟩)=Ctotal\mathcal{C}(|\psi\rangle) + \mathcal{C}(|0\rangle) = \mathcal{C}_{\text{total}}C(∣ψ⟩)+C(∣0⟩)=Ctotal​, with zero relational coherence between system and ancilla (product state).

Step 2 (Putative cloned state). The output ∣ψ⟩⊗∣ψ⟩|\psi\rangle \otimes |\psi\rangle∣ψ⟩⊗∣ψ⟩ would have coherence 2C(∣ψ⟩)+02\mathcal{C}(|\psi\rangle) + 02C(∣ψ⟩)+0 (product state, no relational coherence). For a general state ∣ψ⟩|\psi\rangle∣ψ⟩, the coherence C(∣ψ⟩)\mathcal{C}(|\psi\rangle)C(∣ψ⟩) depends on the state — specifically, on the overlap structure ∣⟨k∣ψ⟩∣2|\langle k | \psi \rangle|^2∣⟨k∣ψ⟩∣2. Since ∣0⟩|0\rangle∣0⟩ is a fixed state, C(∣0⟩)\mathcal{C}(|0\rangle)C(∣0⟩) is a constant, but C(∣ψ⟩)\mathcal{C}(|\psi\rangle)C(∣ψ⟩) varies with ∣ψ⟩|\psi\rangle∣ψ⟩.

Step 3 (Contradiction). For cloning to work for all ∣ψ⟩|\psi\rangle∣ψ⟩, we would need C(∣ψ⟩)+C(∣0⟩)=2C(∣ψ⟩)\mathcal{C}(|\psi\rangle) + \mathcal{C}(|0\rangle) = 2\mathcal{C}(|\psi\rangle)C(∣ψ⟩)+C(∣0⟩)=2C(∣ψ⟩) for all ∣ψ⟩|\psi\rangle∣ψ⟩, which gives C(∣ψ⟩)=C(∣0⟩)=const\mathcal{C}(|\psi\rangle) = \mathcal{C}(|0\rangle) = \text{const}C(∣ψ⟩)=C(∣0⟩)=const. But this is false for a nontrivial Hilbert space: the coherence content of ∣ψ⟩|\psi\rangle∣ψ⟩ relative to any measurement basis varies with the state (a maximally coherent superposition has S=ln⁡dS = \ln dS=lnd while a basis state has S=0S = 0S=0).

A unitary process redistributes coherence but cannot increase the total — so duplicating a state’s full coherence content while maintaining a product-state output is impossible. □\square□

Step 4: Entanglement Monogamy from Coherence Subadditivity

Theorem 4.1 (Monogamy of entanglement). For three observers OA\mathcal{O}_AOA​, OB\mathcal{O}_BOB​, OC\mathcal{O}_COC​ in a joint pure state ∣Ψ⟩ABC|\Psi\rangle_{ABC}∣Ψ⟩ABC​:

S(ρA)≤S(ρAB)+S(ρAC)S(\rho_A) \leq S(\rho_{AB}) + S(\rho_{AC})S(ρA​)≤S(ρAB​)+S(ρAC​)

More precisely, the entanglement between AAA and BBB limits the entanglement between AAA and CCC (and vice versa).

Proof. The argument proceeds from the coherence axioms in three steps.

Step 1 (Strong subadditivity from coherence). By strong subadditivity of the coherence measure (C5 of Coherence Conservation):

C(AB∪C)+C(B)≤C(AB)+C(BC)\mathcal{C}(AB \cup C) + \mathcal{C}(B) \leq \mathcal{C}(AB) + \mathcal{C}(BC)C(AB∪C)+C(B)≤C(AB)+C(BC)

for any three subsystems A,B,CA, B, CA,B,C. By the coherence-entropy correspondence (Theorem 2.1), the relational coherence C(IX)=S(ρX)\mathcal{C}(I_{X}) = S(\rho_X)C(IX​)=S(ρX​) for any subsystem XXX of a pure total state. Translating the strong subadditivity inequality to von Neumann entropy:

S(ρABC)+S(ρB)≤S(ρAB)+S(ρBC)S(\rho_{ABC}) + S(\rho_B) \leq S(\rho_{AB}) + S(\rho_{BC})S(ρABC​)+S(ρB​)≤S(ρAB​)+S(ρBC​)

This is precisely the strong subadditivity of von Neumann entropy (Lieb & Ruskai, 1973). The framework derives this inequality from the coherence axioms rather than from the operator-algebraic properties of the trace.

Step 2 (Monogamy for pure states). For a pure state ∣Ψ⟩ABC|\Psi\rangle_{ABC}∣Ψ⟩ABC​, S(ρABC)=0S(\rho_{ABC}) = 0S(ρABC​)=0 and S(ρBC)=S(ρA)S(\rho_{BC}) = S(\rho_A)S(ρBC​)=S(ρA​) (purification duality: the entropy of a subsystem equals the entropy of its complement for a pure total state). Substituting into the strong subadditivity inequality:

0+S(ρB)≤S(ρAB)+S(ρA)    ⟹    S(ρA)≥S(ρB)−S(ρAB)0 + S(\rho_B) \leq S(\rho_{AB}) + S(\rho_A) \;\;\Longrightarrow\;\; S(\rho_A) \geq S(\rho_B) - S(\rho_{AB})0+S(ρB​)≤S(ρAB​)+S(ρA​)⟹S(ρA​)≥S(ρB​)−S(ρAB​)

Applying the same argument with BBB and CCC exchanged and combining with the Araki-Lieb triangle inequality S(ρAB)≥∣S(ρA)−S(ρB)∣S(\rho_{AB}) \geq |S(\rho_A) - S(\rho_B)|S(ρAB​)≥∣S(ρA​)−S(ρB​)∣ (which follows from subadditivity applied to purifications), we obtain:

S(ρA)≤S(ρAB)+S(ρAC)S(\rho_A) \leq S(\rho_{AB}) + S(\rho_{AC})S(ρA​)≤S(ρAB​)+S(ρAC​)

This is the monogamy inequality: the total entanglement of AAA with BCBCBC is bounded by the sum of AAA‘s entanglement with BBB and with CCC.

Step 3 (CKW tightening for qubits). For qubits (d=2d = 2d=2), the tangle τ=C2\tau = C^2τ=C2 (squared concurrence) provides a sharper monotone:

τ(A:B)+τ(A:C)≤τ(A:BC)\tau(A:B) + \tau(A:C) \leq \tau(A:BC)τ(A:B)+τ(A:C)≤τ(A:BC)

This is the Coffman-Kundu-Wootters inequality (2000). The general entropy-based bound (Step 2) is dimension-independent; the CKW tightening uses the qubit-specific concurrence.

Remark (Physical content). Monogamy means entanglement is a limited resource: committing relational coherence to the AAA-BBB relationship depletes what is available for AAA-CCC. In the coherence picture, this is a direct consequence of conservation (Axiom 1) — coherence cannot be created, only redistributed. The scarcity of entanglement is the scarcity of coherence. □\square□
