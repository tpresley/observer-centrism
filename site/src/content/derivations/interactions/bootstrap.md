## Statement

Relational invariants satisfy the observer definition. They are therefore observers at the next level of the hierarchy. As observers, they participate in further Type III interactions, generating higher-order relational invariants. This self-reinforcing process — the **bootstrap** — is the mechanism by which the universe generates complexity from coherence conservation alone.

## Derivation

### Step 1: Relational Invariants Satisfy the Observer Definition

Let $I_{12}$ be a relational invariant generated by a Type III interaction between $\mathcal{O}_1$ and $\mathcal{O}_2$. We verify it satisfies each component of the observer definition:

**State space.** $I_{12}$ lives on $\Sigma_1 \times \Sigma_2$. Its state space $\Sigma_{12}$ is the submanifold of $\Sigma_1 \times \Sigma_2$ on which $I_{12}$ takes a definite value. This submanifold has at least two elements (since $I_{12}$ is non-constant by assumption).

**Invariant.** $I_{12}$ is itself a conserved quantity (by Axiom 1 — once generated, coherence is conserved). So $I_{12}$ maintains the invariant of its own conservation: the relational invariant *is* the invariant it maintains. $\checkmark$

**Self/non-self distinction.** Transformations that preserve $I_{12}$ are classified as self. Transformations that would disrupt the correlation structure (e.g., decoupling $\mathcal{O}_1$ from $\mathcal{O}_2$ in a way that destroys $I_{12}$) are non-self. The self/non-self distinction is inherited from the interaction that generated $I_{12}$. $\checkmark$

**Loop closure.** The dynamics of $I_{12}$ are determined by the joint dynamics of $\mathcal{O}_1$ and $\mathcal{O}_2$. Since both $\mathcal{O}_1$ and $\mathcal{O}_2$ satisfy loop closure individually, and $I_{12}$ is invariant under their joint dynamics, the projection of the joint loop onto $\Sigma_{12}$ is itself closed. $\checkmark$

Therefore $\mathcal{O}_{12} = (\Sigma_{12}, I_{12}, \mathcal{B}_{12})$ is a well-defined observer.

### Step 2: The Iteration

Since $\mathcal{O}_{12}$ is an observer, it can interact with other observers $\mathcal{O}_3, \mathcal{O}_4, \ldots$ or with other relational invariant observers $\mathcal{O}_{34}, \ldots$ via Type III interactions, generating second-order relational invariants $I_{(12)(34)}$.

The construction is closed: every level of the hierarchy produces valid observers that can participate at the next level. There is no natural stopping point.

$$\mathcal{O}_1, \mathcal{O}_2 \xrightarrow{\text{Type III}} \mathcal{O}_{12} \xrightarrow{\text{Type III with } \mathcal{O}_3} \mathcal{O}_{(12)3} \xrightarrow{\text{Type III}} \cdots$$

### Step 3: The Hierarchy Is Necessary

The bootstrap doesn't merely *permit* hierarchy — it *requires* it. The argument:

**Premise.** Every observer must have a non-trivial self/non-self distinction (from the [Multiplicity](/derivations/minimal-observer/multiplicity) result). This requires non-self transformations sourced by other observers.

**Observation.** Type III interactions between observers generate relational invariants. These relational invariants are new observers (Step 1). These new observers require their own non-trivial environments — which are provided by further interactions, generating further relational invariants.

**Consequence.** The coherence budget does not permit stasis. Every interaction that maintains existing observers also generates new relational structure. The hierarchy grows because coherence conservation drives it:

- Dissolution of an observer returns coherence to the substrate
- The substrate satisfies crystallization conditions (loop closure is available)
- New observers form, carrying the coherence forward
- Their interactions generate further relational invariants

The global coherence is constant, but it is redistributed into ever-richer relational structure. The hierarchy grows in the direction of maximum relational complexity permitted by the coherence budget.

### Step 4: Irreducibility of Higher Levels

The higher-level observers are **irreducibly new** — they cannot be derived from the lower-level description without loss.

*Argument.* The relational invariant $I_{12}$ is, by definition, not expressible as $f(I_1) + g(I_2)$. Any description in terms of $I_1$ and $I_2$ alone loses the relational structure. This is not a limitation of computational power — it is a structural fact about the coherence geometry.

**Consequence for reductionism.** The higher-level sciences (chemistry, biology, neuroscience) are not in principle reducible to particle physics. Each level introduces genuinely new relational invariants that do not exist at the lower level. The irreducibility is ontological, not merely epistemological.

## The Two Boundaries

The hierarchy has a floor and a ceiling:

**Floor**: The pre-observational coherence geometry — the substrate from which the first stable loops crystallize. This is the quantum vacuum.

**Ceiling**: The universe as a whole satisfies the observer definition (it maintains total coherence, has a self/non-self distinction in the laws of physics, and its dynamics close). But no observer *within* the universe can reach this level — it is approached asymptotically but never contained by any bounded observer.

## Rigor Assessment

**What is established:**
- The verification that $\mathcal{O}_{12}$ satisfies the observer definition is explicit (state space, invariant, self/non-self, loop closure each verified)
- The closure of the construction under iteration is clear
- The irreducibility claim follows from the definition of relational invariants

**What needs work:**
- The loop closure step (joint loop projects to closed loop on $\Sigma_{12}$) assumes the projection is well-behaved — needs a topological argument
- "The hierarchy is necessary" is argued informally; a formal proof would need to show that a static configuration (hierarchy that doesn't grow) is dynamically unstable
- The asymptotic ceiling claim needs formalization

## Open Gaps

1. **Growth rate**: How fast does the hierarchy grow? Is there a characteristic rate of relational invariant generation per interaction? This connects to the cosmological history of complexity.
2. **Stability**: Not every relational invariant generated will be stable. The persistent hierarchy consists only of those relational invariants whose loops close stably. What fraction of generated relational invariants are stable?
3. **The fixed-point equation**: The universe as its own observer, maintaining self-consistency, is the fixed point of the bootstrap. Stating this as a mathematical fixed-point equation is a major formalization target.
