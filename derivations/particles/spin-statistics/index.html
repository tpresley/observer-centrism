Statement

Theorem. In three spatial dimensions, there are exactly two topological classes of observer loop, determined by π1(SO(3))=Z2\pi_1(SO(3)) = \mathbb{Z}_2π1​(SO(3))=Z2​. These two classes correspond to the two exchange statistics (symmetric and antisymmetric) and to the two spin classes (integer and half-integer). The spin-statistics connection is the identification of these two descriptions of the same topological invariant.

Derivation

Structural Postulate

Structural Postulate S1 (Topological consistency). Transition amplitudes are single-valued functions on the universal cover of the configuration space. That is, the amplitude for a process depends on the homotopy class of the path in configuration space, not just the endpoints. Equivalently, the wave function is a section of a flat line bundle over the configuration space, with holonomy group Hom(π1(Q),U(1))\text{Hom}(\pi_1(Q), U(1))Hom(π1​(Q),U(1)).

Remark. This is the standard quantization condition in quantum mechanics on multiply-connected spaces (Laidlaw-DeWitt, 1971). In the framework, it follows from the requirement that phase evolution along paths in the interaction graph is well-defined: distinct homotopy classes in the configuration space can accumulate different U(1)U(1)U(1) phases, but the phase must be single-valued on the covering space. This is loop closure (Axiom 3) applied to the configuration space rather than to physical space.

Step 1: Observer Loops in the Rotation Group

Definition 1.1. A minimal observer loop in d=3d = 3d=3 spatial dimensions is a continuous map γ:S1→SO(3)\gamma: S^1 \to SO(3)γ:S1→SO(3) representing the observer’s orientation cycle. Two loops γ0,γ1\gamma_0, \gamma_1γ0​,γ1​ are equivalent if they are homotopic: there exists a continuous H:S1×[0,1]→SO(3)H: S^1 \times [0,1] \to SO(3)H:S1×[0,1]→SO(3) with H(−,0)=γ0H(-,0) = \gamma_0H(−,0)=γ0​ and H(−,1)=γ1H(-,1) = \gamma_1H(−,1)=γ1​.

The set of equivalence classes is the fundamental group π1(SO(3))\pi_1(SO(3))π1​(SO(3)).

Proposition 1.2. π1(SO(3))=Z2\pi_1(SO(3)) = \mathbb{Z}_2π1​(SO(3))=Z2​.

Proof. The group SO(3)SO(3)SO(3) is diffeomorphic to RP3\mathbb{RP}^3RP3 (real projective 3-space).   • Class [0][0][0]: Loops homotopic to the constant map (contractible). These lift to closed loops in SU(2)SU(2)SU(2).

Step 5: Anyons in Two Dimensions

Step 6: Completeness — Why No Other Statistics Exist in d=3d = 3d=3

Theorem 6.1. In d=3d = 3d=3 spatial dimensions, the only exchange statistics consistent with the topology of the configuration space are symmetric (bosonic) and antisymmetric (fermionic). No other statistics — including parastatistics, fractional statistics, or infinite statistics — are topologically consistent.

Proof. The fundamental group π1(Q2)=Z2\pi_1(Q_2) = \mathbb{Z}_2π1​(Q2​)=Z2​ has exactly two one-dimensional unitary representations:

  • The trivial representation: exchange →+1\to +1→+1 (bosonic)
  • The sign representation: exchange →−1\to -1→−1 (fermionic)

Higher-dimensional representations of Z2\mathbb{Z}_2Z2​ decompose into direct sums of these two. Parastatistics (which would require representations of the symmetric group SnS_nSn​ for nnn particles) reduce to ordinary bosonic/fermionic statistics in d=3d = 3d=3 by the theorem of Doplicher, Haag, and Roberts (1971, 1974), which establishes that all parastatistical sectors are equivalent to collections of ordinary bosons and fermions in the presence of a gauge symmetry. □\square□

Open Gaps

  1. Minimal spin: Why is the minimal fermion spin-1/2 and not spin-3/2? The answer is that s=1/2s = 1/2s=1/2 is the fundamental (lowest-dimensional) representation of SU(2)SU(2)SU(2), and the minimal observer has the simplest possible loop.
