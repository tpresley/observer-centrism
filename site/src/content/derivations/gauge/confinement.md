## Statement

**Goal.** Show that quarks are permanently confined into color-singlet hadrons as a consequence of the non-associative structure of the octonions underlying SU(3) color.

The derivation should:

1. Show that non-associativity of $\mathbb{O}$ prevents consistent long-range parallel transport of colored states
2. Prove that only color-singlet combinations (where non-associative phases cancel) can propagate to asymptotic distances
3. Derive the linear confining potential $V(r) \sim \sigma r$ at large distances from coherence string tension
4. Recover asymptotic freedom at short distances where the non-associativity effects are subdominant
5. Derive the string tension $\sigma$ in terms of framework parameters

## Dependencies

This derivation requires:
- [Color Force](/derivations/gauge/color-force) — the octonionic origin of SU(3) and its non-associative structure

## Open Questions

1. Can the derivation produce a quantitative value for the string tension?
2. How does the deconfinement phase transition (quark-gluon plasma) emerge?
3. What is the relationship between the non-associative confinement mechanism and the Wilson loop criterion?
4. Can the explicit gluon spectrum (glueball masses) be derived?
