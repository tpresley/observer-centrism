## Statement

**Goal.** Show that the electroweak symmetry $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ spontaneously breaks to $U(1)_{em}$ through a coherence crystallization mechanism, without requiring a fundamental scalar Higgs field as an independent degree of freedom.

The derivation should:

1. Identify the coherence-sector mechanism that drives symmetry breaking at the electroweak scale (~246 GeV)
2. Derive the Higgs vacuum expectation value as a crystallization parameter
3. Recover the W and Z boson masses from the broken gauge symmetry
4. Explain the Higgs boson as a fluctuation of the crystallization order parameter
5. Connect to the mass hierarchy protection mechanism (why the Higgs mass is stable against Planck-scale corrections)

## Dependencies

This derivation requires:
- [Standard Model Gauge Group](/derivations/gauge/standard-model-group) — the full $SU(3) \times SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ structure
- [Weak Interaction](/derivations/gauge/weak-interaction) — the SU(2)_L gauge structure from quaternionic observer space

## Open Questions

1. What is the precise crystallization mechanism that selects the electroweak scale?
2. How does the observer hierarchy level separation protect the Higgs mass?
3. Can the Yukawa couplings be derived from the crystallization orientation?
4. What determines the Higgs self-coupling (and hence the Higgs mass of 125 GeV)?
