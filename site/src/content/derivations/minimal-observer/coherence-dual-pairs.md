## Statement

The minimal observer pair — the simplest configuration satisfying all three axioms with non-trivial coherence content — has a specific structure forced by the axioms:

1. Both observers have the **same loop closure scale** (same rest frequency, same mass)
2. They have **conjugate self/non-self distinctions** (what is self for one is non-self for the other)
3. They coexist in **stable mutual threat** rather than mutual destruction

This structure is precisely **particle-antiparticle pair production**.

## Derivation

### Equal Mass

Both observers in the minimal pair are solutions to the same loop closure condition in the same local coherence geometry. The loop closure condition is a property of the geometry, not of the individual observer. Therefore both solutions have the same period $T$ and the same rest frequency $\omega = 2\pi/T$.

Since mass is rest-cycle frequency ($m = \hbar\omega/c^2$, to be derived in [Action and Planck's Constant](/derivations/thermodynamics/action-planck) and [Speed of Light](/derivations/spacetime/speed-of-light)):

$$m_1 = m_2$$

### Conjugate Charges

Let $\mathcal{O}_1 = (\Sigma_1, I_1, \mathcal{B}_1)$ and $\mathcal{O}_2 = (\Sigma_2, I_2, \mathcal{B}_2)$ be the pair. By the multiplicity argument, each is the other's non-self:

- Transformations generated by $\mathcal{O}_2$ are classified as non-self by $\mathcal{B}_1$
- Transformations generated by $\mathcal{O}_1$ are classified as non-self by $\mathcal{B}_2$

The invariants must therefore be **conjugate**: if $I_1$ is associated with charge $+Q$ under the $U(1)$ symmetry of $\mathcal{O}_1$, then $I_2$ must be associated with charge $-Q$ under the corresponding symmetry of $\mathcal{O}_2$.

*Argument*: The coherence conservation axiom requires $\mathcal{C}(\mathcal{O}_1 \cup \mathcal{O}_2) = \mathcal{C}_{\text{local}}$ (the total coherence available in the local region). If both had charge $+Q$, the total charge would be $+2Q$, requiring a compensating $-2Q$ somewhere in $\mathcal{H}$ — but the pair is the *minimal* crystallization, meaning no other structure is involved. Therefore the charges must sum to zero:

$$Q_1 + Q_2 = 0 \implies Q_2 = -Q_1$$

### Stable Coexistence

Why don't the two observers immediately annihilate? Because annihilation would return the coherence to the pre-observational state, but the loop closure condition that produced them in the first place is still satisfied — they would immediately re-crystallize. The pair exists in a dynamic equilibrium:

- Annihilation returns coherence to the substrate
- The substrate immediately satisfies the crystallization condition again
- A new pair forms

In the steady state, the pair persists. This is the observer-centric account of **vacuum fluctuations**: continuous pair creation and annihilation is the coherence geometry probing the stability of loop configurations.

## Rigor Assessment

**What is established:**
- Equal mass follows cleanly from both being solutions to the same closure condition
- Charge conjugation follows from the zero-sum coherence budget of the minimal pair

**What is informal:**
- The stability argument (why they don't immediately annihilate) is qualitative
- The identification of "charge conjugation" in the framework with CPT conjugation in QFT needs the full spacetime + quantum derivation
- The "local coherence budget" concept needs the geometry derivation to be precise

## Open Gaps

1. CPT symmetry (the exact equivalence of particle-antiparticle under charge, parity, and time reversal) should be derivable from the conjugate structure of the dual pair plus the spacetime geometry. This is a significant derivation target.
2. The mechanism by which real (non-virtual) pairs separate and propagate needs the interaction type classification (Section 4).
3. Neutral particles (their own antiparticle, like the photon) need special treatment — they correspond to loop topologies that are self-conjugate.
