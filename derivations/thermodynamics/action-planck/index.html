Statement

Theorem. Action is the coherence cost of a transformation in the coherence geometry. Planck’s constant ℏ\hbarℏ is the minimum coherence cost of one observer cycle — the irreducible quantum of action, fixed by the compactness of the minimal observer’s state space. The principle of stationary action and the Heisenberg uncertainty principle both follow from this identification.

Structural Postulate

S1 (Smooth coherence measure). The coherence measure C\mathcal{C}C, restricted to any observer state space Σ\SigmaΣ, is at least C2C^2C2 (twice continuously differentiable). This extends the structural postulate S2 of Loop Closure (which provides a GOG_\mathcal{O}GO​-invariant Riemannian metric on Σ\SigmaΣ) by requiring that the Hessian of C\mathcal{C}C itself yields a positive-definite metric on the physical state space.

Remark. The GOG_\mathcal{O}GO​-invariant metric from Loop Closure (S2) and the Hessian metric from C\mathcal{C}C are structurally related: both measure the cost of motion on Σ\SigmaΣ. The postulate is that they agree — i.e., the natural metric on Σ\SigmaΣ is the one induced by the coherence measure. This is a uniqueness assumption that restricts the framework to a single geometric structure on state space.
