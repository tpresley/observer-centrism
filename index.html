Observer-Centrism

Physics from Three Axioms

Why is science possible? Not in the philosophical sense — in the structural sense. The universe is not merely describable by mathematics; it is unreasonably well-described by it. Physical law has a compressibility, a pattern-density, that demands explanation. Whatever the universe fundamentally is, it must be the kind of thing that admits observers capable of discovering its regularities.

That observation is usually treated as a curiosity. Observer-Centrism treats it as a starting point — and shows that taking it seriously, with mathematical precision, leads somewhere unexpected.

The Problem

Modern physics is built from four frameworks: quantum mechanics, general relativity, the Standard Model, and thermodynamics. Each is spectacularly successful in its domain. None is complete. They are mathematically incompatible at the Planck scale. The Standard Model alone carries nineteen free parameters — measured but unexplained. The measurement problem, the origin of the arrow of time, and the hierarchy of particle masses remain open after decades. These aren't loose ends. They are symptoms of missing foundations.

The Claim

Observer-Centrism demonstrates that three axioms — conservation of a primitive quantity, a structural definition of what counts as an observer, and the requirement of dynamical stability — are sufficient to derive quantum mechanics, general relativity, the Standard Model gauge group, thermodynamics, holographic entropy bounds, and the three-generation particle spectrum. Not as assumptions, but as mathematical consequences.

The entire framework rests on three commitments:

Axiom 1 — Coherence Conservation

There exists a primitive quantity — coherence — that is globally conserved. It cannot be created from nothing, destroyed, or exported to an external reservoir. The ontology is closed.

Axiom 2 — Observer Definition

Any structure that maintains at least one invariant quantity across transformations, together with a distinction between self-preserving and self-threatening transformations, qualifies as an observer.

Axiom 3 — Loop Closure

Observer dynamics must be closed loops: the internal state returns, at least approximately, to its initial configuration after a finite time. Non-closing configurations dissipate.

What Follows

From these three axioms, a chain of 40 rigorous derivations produces the major structures of known physics — each step following by mathematical necessity from the steps before it. The Standard Model gauge group U(1) × SU(2) × SU(3) emerges from the four normed division algebras. Spacetime curvature follows from the relational structure of interacting observers. The Born rule, Pauli exclusion, and three generations of fermions all emerge without being assumed.

The framework also makes testable predictions that distinguish it from existing theories: a specific angular signature in holographic noise measurable with co-located interferometers, a granularity scale in dark matter halos with a distinctive power-spectrum cutoff, and the non-convergence of gauge couplings at any energy — contradicting all grand unified theories.
