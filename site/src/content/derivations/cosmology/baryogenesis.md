## Target

**Explain the observed baryon asymmetry $\eta_B \approx 6 \times 10^{-10}$ (the matter-antimatter imbalance) from the framework's axioms and gauge structure.**

The Coherence-Dual Pairs derivation shows that particle-antiparticle pairs crystallize symmetrically — equal and opposite charges. Yet the universe is overwhelmingly matter, not antimatter. The Sakharov conditions (1967) require: (1) baryon number violation, (2) C and CP violation, (3) departure from thermal equilibrium. The framework must identify mechanisms for all three.

## Prior Work

The earlier whitepaper (idea #25) proposed baryogenesis from SU(2)$_R$ precession: early-universe holonomy in the quaternionic structure produces axial bias $\eta_B \approx 6 \times 10^{-10}$. This specific mechanism depends on the whitepaper's quaternionic formalism.

The transferable insight: the framework's coherence-dual pairs crystallize symmetrically in vacuum, but early-universe dynamics (non-equilibrium coherence redistribution) can break this symmetry. The SU(2) gauge structure provides the necessary C and CP violation through chiral coupling.

## Proposed Approach

1. **Sakharov condition 1 (baryon number violation)**: SU(2) gauge theories have sphaleron processes that violate baryon + lepton number (B+L) while conserving B-L. If the weak interaction is derived from the framework (Weak Interaction stub), sphalerons follow automatically.
2. **Sakharov condition 2 (CP violation)**: The framework's coherence-dual pairs have exact charge conjugation symmetry in the ground state. CP violation requires the gauge sector to distinguish matter from antimatter. The CKM/PMNS CP phases (Flavor Mixing stub) provide this.
3. **Sakharov condition 3 (non-equilibrium)**: The bootstrap hierarchy's formation during the early universe provides the departure from equilibrium. As successively higher levels of the hierarchy crystallize, the universe passes through phase transitions.
4. **Compute $\eta_B$**: The sphaleron rate, CP-violating phase, and departure from equilibrium combine to give $\eta_B$. The framework should constrain these ingredients from its axioms.

### Structural Postulate Needed

Likely none beyond those of the prerequisite derivations (Weak Interaction, Flavor Mixing). Baryogenesis should be a consequence of the gauge structure and cosmological dynamics.

## Key Challenges

1. **Depends on unfinished derivations**: This stub requires the Weak Interaction and Flavor Mixing derivations to be completed first. It is deep in the dependency chain.
2. **Quantitative prediction**: Computing $\eta_B$ to the observed precision ($6 \times 10^{-10}$) requires detailed knowledge of the electroweak phase transition, which depends on the Higgs mechanism (not yet derived).
3. **Electroweak baryogenesis vs. leptogenesis**: Standard Model CP violation may be insufficient for the observed asymmetry. The framework might need leptogenesis (B-L violation at high scales) instead. This depends on whether neutrinos are Majorana or Dirac (an open question in the Three Generations derivation).
4. **Temperature and timescales**: The framework derives time from phase ordering (thermodynamics/time), but early-universe thermodynamics at the electroweak scale ($T \sim 100$ GeV) requires the full gauge structure.
