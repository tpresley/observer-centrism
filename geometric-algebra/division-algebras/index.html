The Clifford algebra perspective developed here provides a complementary viewpoint. Step 1: Two Doubling Constructions

There are two natural ways to build algebras by doubling: Cayley-Dickson (division algebra doubling) and Clifford (geometric algebra construction). They start the same, diverge, and reconnect through complexification.

This doubles the dimension and preserves the norm ∣xy∣=∣x∣∣y∣|xy| = |x||y|∣xy∣=∣x∣∣y∣, but sacrifices one algebraic property at each step:

Clifford construction. Given a vector space VVV with quadratic form QQQ, the Clifford algebra Cl⁡(V,Q)\operatorname{Cl}(V,Q)Cl(V,Q) is the associative algebra generated by VVV subject to v2=Q(v)v^2 = Q(v)v2=Q(v). Step 3: The Octonionic Exception

The pattern of Step 2 breaks at the octonions.

Proposition 3.1 (Octonions are not a Clifford algebra). The octonion algebra O\mathbb{O}O is not isomorphic to any Clifford algebra Cl⁡(p,q)\operatorname{Cl}(p,q)Cl(p,q) or even subalgebra Cl⁡+(p,q)\operatorname{Cl}^+(p,q)Cl+(p,q).

Proof. All Clifford algebras are associative by construction. The octonions are non-associative: for example, (e1e2)e4=e4e3e4≠e1(e2e4)(e_1 e_2)e_4 = e_4 e_3 e_4 \neq e_1(e_2 e_4)(e1​e2​)e4​=e4​e3​e4​=e1​(e2​e4​) in the Fano plane multiplication. Since associativity is a property preserved under isomorphism, no associative algebra can be isomorphic to O\mathbb{O}O. □\square□

This is the fundamental divergence point. The Cayley-Dickson construction, which the framework’s bootstrap mechanism forces (Theorem 2.3 of Bootstrap Division Algebras), produces a non-associative algebra at the third step. The Clifford construction, which always preserves associativity, cannot directly produce the octonions. The gap is bridged by complexification.

Step 6: Associativity Divergence — The Core Structural Point

Step 7: Termination — Clifford vs Cayley-Dickson

The framework’s bootstrap derivation terminates at O\mathbb{O}O because sedenions have zero divisors (Theorem 7.1). What does this look like from the Clifford side?

Limitations (honest assessment):
