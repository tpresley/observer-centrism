Overview

The framework rejects a shared global background. Every observer has its own ticks (Loop Closure), its own self/non-self boundary (Observer Definition), and its own horizon (Horizon Gauge Shell Prop 3.2, remark “Observer-universal, not just cosmological”). Observer Loop Viability Step 8 already commits to a level-indexed effective Λneff\Lambda_n^{\text{eff}}Λneff​ — each bootstrap level projects its own effective geometry.

This derivation makes the consequence explicit: there is no single continuous dual of the DAG. There is an observer-indexed family of continuous duals, one per observer, with structure determined by how the members of the family relate on shared Type III relations.

The program splits naturally into two tracks, which turn out to be two halves of a single object:

  1. Stalks (what each observer projects). For a given observer AAA, the intrinsic DAG-level constraints on AAA — Axiom 3 periodicity, Axiom 2 boundary, a null horizon shell at cTA/2c T_A / 2cTA​/2, and smooth Lorentzian interior — determine AAA‘s projected patch MAM_AMA​ up to coordinates. For a minimal observer, Theorem 3.1 (below) proves the answer is the static patch of de Sitter space with de Sitter radius LA=cTA/2L_A = c T_A / 2LA​=cTA​/2, decorated with Type III partner lines that pierce the interior and terminate on the horizon as null geodesics. The proof invokes the classical Birkhoff theorem with positive cosmological constant plus a framework-internal lemma (Lemma 3.0) that the Schwarzschild mass vanishes on the observer’s own projection.

  2. Gluing (how the stalks relate). For observers A,BA, BA,B sharing a Type III relation IABI_{AB}IAB​, there is a restriction condition: the portion of MAM_AMA​ carrying IABI_{AB}IAB​ must match the portion of MBM_BMB​ carrying the same relation. The collection {MA}\{M_A\}{MA​} with restriction maps forms a presheaf over the observer category. Whether it is a sheaf — i.e., whether local agreement on overlaps suffices — is a property of the framework, not a definition.

Why this reframes long-standing puzzles. The cosmological constant problem (120 orders of magnitude between Planck and observed Λ\LambdaΛ) has resisted derivation because the question “what is Λ\LambdaΛ?” presupposes a shared background for Λ\LambdaΛ to live in. Here Λ\LambdaΛ is observer-indexed: level-nnn observers project Λn=12/(cTn)2\Lambda_n = 12/(c T_n)^2Λn​=12/(cTn​)2. The hierarchy between Λ0\Lambda_0Λ0​ (Planck) and ΛN\Lambda_NΛN​ (cosmic) is not a bug; it is the obstruction class of the presheaf’s failure to reduce to a single manifold across levels. The 120 orders are a measurement of gluing failure, not of fine-tuning.

Honest status. The minimal-observer projection is established rigorously (Theorem 3.1) via Birkhoff-with-Λ\LambdaΛ plus Lemma 3.0 (MASchw=0M_A^{\text{Schw}} = 0MASchw​=0 proved by Kretschmann-singularity contradiction against Axiom 3). The gluing/sheaf property (Conjecture 5.3) and the quantitative obstruction class (Proposition 6.3) are genuinely conjectural; these are flagged as the remaining open gaps. The derivation’s status remains provisional rather than derived because it inherits the active postulate speed-of-light-s1 transitively through Einstein Field Equations — a global-assembly commitment that this derivation itself does not use but cannot shed upstream.

Definition 5.1 (Restriction maps on shared relations). The assignment A↦MAA \mapsto M_AA↦MA​ together with restriction maps ρAB\rho_{AB}ρAB​ for every shared Type III relation forms a contravariant presheaf M:Obsop→LorPatchM: \mathbf{Obs}^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{LorPatch}M:Obsop→LorPatch. ρAC=ρBC∘ρAB\rho_{AC} = \rho_{BC} \circ \rho_{AB}ρAC​=ρBC​∘ρAB​

on the common portion IAB∩IBC∩IACI_{AB} \cap I_{BC} \cap I_{AC}IAB​∩IBC​∩IAC​.

Structural argument. A presheaf is a contravariant functor; contravariance here reflects that ρAB\rho_{AB}ρAB​ goes from AAA‘s view to BBB‘s view along the direction of relation composition. The cocycle condition is the standard composition law: if AAA and BBB agree on their view of IABI_{AB}IAB​, and BBB and CCC agree on their view of IBCI_{BC}IBC​, then AAA and CCC should agree on the portion of the DAG accessible through the composite relation. This is the direct analogue of the triangle condition in sheaf theory.

Conjecture 5.3 (Sheaf property). The presheaf MMM is a sheaf: for any covering family of Type III relations on an observer network, local agreement on each relation determines the global projection on the union. Equivalently, the cocycle condition is not only necessary but sufficient to reconstruct the observer-indexed family from its restrictions.

Status. Unproved. The sheaf property is delicate in the present setting because (a) the covering is by Type III relations rather than open sets, (b) the presheaf takes values in Lorentzian patches rather than abelian groups, and (c) the framework’s DAG is not a topological space in the standard sense. A proof would require a categorical-semantics formulation of “cover” on Obs\mathbf{Obs}Obs. This is flagged as Open Gap 3.

Remark 6.4 (Not a solution, a reframing). Proposition 6.3 does not compute any Λ\LambdaΛ from first principles. Consequences

1. Holographic bound is per-observer, not global. Each MAM_AMA​ has its own holographic bound Nmax(A)=πLA2/ℓP2N_{\text{max}}^{(A)} = \pi L_A^2/\ell_P^2Nmax(A)​=πLA2​/ℓP2​. 3. Horizon content is specific to the observer. The null shell carries only Type III relations that cross ∂MA\partial M_A∂MA​.
