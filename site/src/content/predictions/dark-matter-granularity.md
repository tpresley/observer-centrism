## Prediction

If dark matter consists of stable observer loops, it must have a characteristic coherence domain size set by its loop closure scale. This implies a **minimum granularity**: a minimum dark matter halo mass below which dark matter structures do not form, set by quantum pressure from the loop closure condition.

## Derivation Sketch

1. Dark matter particles are stable observer loops (by universality of the observer definition)
2. Each loop has a coherence domain with characteristic size $\lambda_{DM}$
3. The loop closure condition imposes a minimum spatial scale for coherent structure
4. Below this scale, quantum pressure from loop closure prevents gravitational collapse
5. Therefore: minimum halo mass $M_{\min} \sim \rho_{DM} \lambda_{DM}^3$

## Why This Is Distinctive

Most dark matter models (WIMPs, axions) predict a minimum halo mass, but the *mechanism* here is different: it comes from the loop closure condition rather than from thermal freeze-out or field oscillation scales. The specific relationship between $\lambda_{DM}$, the dark matter mass, and $M_{\min}$ could in principle distinguish this prediction.

## Testability

Observable via:
- Suppression of small-scale structure in dwarf galaxy populations
- Gravitational lensing substructure measurements
- The matter power spectrum cutoff at small scales
- Stellar stream gaps from dark matter subhalo encounters
