Extension Papers: Idea Analysis

Alongside the main whitepaper, four extension papers explored more speculative directions within the earlier Observer-Centrism framework. These papers used the same foundation — the OC action, Fisher information metric, A₅ icosahedral geometry, and functional renormalization group — to address neutrino flavor structure, gauge coupling predictions, self-duality, and a complementarity principle.

These papers are more speculative than the core whitepaper, and their results depend heavily on the specific OC action formalism. This analysis catalogs their distinct ideas, assesses coverage in the current rigorous framework, and evaluates viability for absorption — judged, as before, by whether an idea is plausibly derivable from the current axioms and derivation chain.

1

Source–flux duality of the quadratic OC action

Not covered Low
Self-Dual A₅ Geometry (§1–2)

The quadratic sector of the OC action admits an exact 0-form ↔ 2-form duality via a parent action with auxiliary 3-forms. Varying one way recovers the scalar action; varying the other gives a dual 2-form action. For the massless case, this is exact scalar–2-form duality.

Coverage in current framework

The current framework does not use a parent action formalism or construct duality transformations. The action-planck derivation identifies action with coherence cost but does not explore duality structure.

Viability for absorption

This duality is specific to the OC action S_OC with its Fisher metric kinetic term and KL potential — neither of which appear in the current framework. The current framework derives field equations from structural constraints (Lovelock, Noether) rather than from a specific Lagrangian, so there is no action whose duality structure could be analyzed. The mathematical technique (parent action with auxiliary forms) is standard in p-form gauge theory and could be applied if an explicit action were constructed, but that is not the current framework's approach.

A₅ icosahedral–dodecahedral pair as discrete Hodge complex

Not covered Low
Self-Dual A₅ Geometry (§4), Plateau Equivalence (§4)

The icosahedron (12 vertices, 20 faces) and dodecahedron (20 vertices, 12 faces) form a minimal isotropic primal–dual pair on the 2-sphere. Their stiffness operators approximate the continuous Hodge star, with primal–dual energy matching to machine precision on the self-dual blend.

Coverage in current framework

The current framework does not use discrete geometry, Platonic solids, or discrete Hodge theory. Its geometry derives from smooth manifold structure (pseudo-Riemannian metrics, Lovelock theorem) and topology (winding classes, homotopy groups).

Viability for absorption

The A₅ discrete Hodge complex is a computational tool for discretizing the continuum OC action, not a fundamental structure. The current framework works with smooth geometry throughout and has no need for lattice discretization at the foundational level. The icosahedral symmetry group A₅ is interesting mathematically (it is the largest finite simple group with a faithful 3D representation), but connecting it to the current framework's topological approach would require showing why discrete approximation is physically meaningful rather than a numerical convenience.

Within the current framework, the three-generations derivation shows that generations correspond to independent winding axes in 3D. If these axes have slightly different coherence costs (analogous to the Fisher curvature difference), a breaking tensor could emerge. The challenge is that the current framework's winding-axis picture is topological, not geometric in the Fisher-metric sense, so the connection requires bridging two different mathematical languages.

Viability for absorption

If the coherence cost function on the space of winding configurations has A₅ symmetry (which is plausible given three dimensions and the uniqueness of A₅ as a non-abelian finite simple subgroup of SO(3)), residual symmetry selection could work within the framework. This is one of the more promising ideas for connecting to experiment.

Not covered Medium
Neutrino Flavor (§2, §4.2)

Coverage in current framework

The current framework does not have an explicit mechanism for flavor symmetry breaking or a breaking parameter. Mass hierarchies come from exponential tunneling suppression in the bootstrap mechanism, which is a different structure.

Viability for absorption

The idea that flavor breaking has a natural geometric scale (order-unity when measured against the breaking tensor norm) is attractive and avoids fine-tuning. Within the current framework, the mass hierarchy derivation already explains why masses span many orders of magnitude through exponential tunneling. If the flavor-breaking tensor D could be identified with a tunneling barrier anisotropy, κ would map to the tunneling rate — potentially connecting the two approaches. The order-unity naturalness claim would then follow from the bootstrap mechanism's fixed-point structure.

It has mild cylindrical degeneracy (λ₁ ≈ λ₂) and its scalar invariants are rotation-stable to ppm — a bona fide geometric tensor.

Coverage in current framework

No analog in the current framework. The breaking tensor concept requires the Fisher metric and icosahedral geometry.

Viability for absorption

The specific numerical eigenstructure of D depends entirely on the icosahedral Fisher geometry. While the qualitative feature (mild hierarchy among eigenvalues, approximate cylindrical symmetry) could be a useful constraint on any flavor model, the specific numbers are not transferable to the current framework. The 'nearly diagonal' structure and ppm-stable scalar invariants suggest genuine geometric content, but verifying whether an analog exists in the winding-axis picture would require constructing the coherence cost anisotropy on the space of winding configurations — a non-trivial calculation.
