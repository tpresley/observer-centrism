STA provides natural geometric language for wormhole throat geometry. The throat is a minimal bivector surface. The duality between EPR (bivector entanglement) and ER (rotor-generated curvature) becomes a structural equivalence within $\operatorname{Cl}(1,3)$. The framework's derivation connects entanglement to spacetime topology via coherence channels; GA may make this connection algebraically explicit.

## Connection to Framework Derivation

The target derivation [ER=EPR from Relational Invariants](/derivations/holography/er-epr) has status **rigorous**. It proves that relational invariants between spatially separated observers manifest as both entanglement (EPR) and non-traversable wormholes (ER), with the throat area satisfying $A_{\text{ER}} = 4\,\ell_P^2\,S_{\text{ent}}$. Non-traversability follows from the no-signaling property of relational invariants.

The GA exploration asks whether the STA formulation of wormhole geometry makes the ER=EPR duality more transparent as a single algebraic structure.

## GA Structure

Primary algebra: $\operatorname{Cl}(1,3)$ (Spacetime Algebra).

Key GA objects to develop:

1. **Bivector entanglement** — EPR correlations encoded as grade-2 elements $B \in \bigwedge^2 \operatorname{Cl}(1,3)$ shared between two observer algebras
2. **Rotor-generated curvature** — the wormhole throat as a region where the rotor field $R(x)$ has non-trivial topology, with $\tilde{R}\,\partial_\mu\,R$ encoding the connection
3. **Minimal bivector surface** — the throat area as the minimum of $\|B\|$ over a cross-section, linking to the $A = 4\,\ell_P^2\,S_{\text{ent}}$ relation
4. **Non-traversability condition** — expressed as a constraint on the rotor field: the throat rotor has unit determinant but no timelike component, blocking causal signals
