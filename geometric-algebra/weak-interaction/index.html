Assessment: What GA Adds

Genuine simplifications:

  1. Quaternions demystified. The quaternion algebra H\mathbb{H}H, which may seem like an exotic mathematical structure, is simply the even subalgebra of 3D Euclidean Clifford algebra. The three quaternion units are the three basis bivectors — the three independent rotation planes in 3D space. This makes the target derivation’s Theorem 2.1 (quaternionic closure) self-evident in Clifford terms: three independent rotation planes automatically generate Cl⁡+(3,0)\operatorname{Cl}^+(3,0)Cl+(3,0).

  2. Gauge transformations are rotors. The SU(2)SU(2)SU(2) gauge transformation ψ→g(x)ψ\psi \to g(x)\psiψ→g(x)ψ in matrix notation becomes ψ→R(x)ψ\psi \to R(x)\psiψ→R(x)ψ in Clifford notation. The Clifford version uses only the geometric product and reversion — no matrices, no complex iii, no inverse operation.

  3. Self-interaction from one product. Why do WWW bosons self-interact while photons do not? Because distinct bivectors do not commute under the geometric product (e23e31≠e31e23e_{23}e_{31} \neq e_{31}e_{23}e23​e31​=e31​e23​), while a single bivector commutes with itself (e12e12=−1e_{12}e_{12} = -1e12​e12​=−1, a scalar).   4. Bootstrap doubling = adding a dimension. The Cayley-Dickson chain C→H\mathbb{C} \to \mathbb{H}C→H corresponds to Cl⁡+(2,0)→Cl⁡+(3,0)\operatorname{Cl}^+(2,0) \to \operatorname{Cl}^+(3,0)Cl+(2,0)→Cl+(3,0) — adding one spatial direction. The entire bootstrap hierarchy becomes: each new spatial dimension adds new bivector planes, enlarging the phase algebra and the gauge group.

Genuine insights:

  1. Weak interaction and spin share the same algebra. The rotor group of Cl⁡+(3,0)\operatorname{Cl}^+(3,0)Cl+(3,0) is simultaneously Spin⁡(3)\operatorname{Spin}(3)Spin(3) (the double cover of spatial rotations, giving spin) and SU(2)SU(2)SU(2) (the weak gauge group).
