## Statement

The Einstein field equations $G_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G \, T_{\mu\nu}$ express the requirement that the coherence geometry be globally self-consistent. The curvature generated by observers (left side) must be consistent with the energy-momentum distribution of those observers (right side), which is itself determined by the trajectories they follow in the curved geometry. The Einstein equations are the **fixed-point conditions** of this self-referential loop.

## Derivation

### Step 1: The Self-Consistency Requirement

From [Gravity](/derivations/spacetime/gravity), the coherence geometry $g_{\mu\nu}$ is determined by the relational invariant density, which is determined by the distribution of observers, which move along geodesics of the geometry $g_{\mu\nu}$. This is a self-referential loop:

$$\text{geometry} \xrightarrow{\text{geodesics}} \text{observer trajectories} \xrightarrow{\text{relational invariants}} \text{density} \xrightarrow{\text{curvature}} \text{geometry}$$

A valid physical configuration is one where this loop is **self-consistent** — a fixed point where the geometry generates trajectories that generate densities that reproduce the geometry.

### Step 2: The Energy-Momentum Tensor

The **energy-momentum tensor** $T_{\mu\nu}$ encodes the distribution and flow of coherence content in the coherence geometry:

- $T_{00}$: coherence density (energy density) — the total coherence content per unit volume
- $T_{0i}$: coherence flux (momentum density) — the flow of coherence through the geometry
- $T_{ij}$: coherence stress — the flux of coherence-flux (pressure, shear)

**Conservation.** From Axiom 1 (Coherence Conservation):

$$\nabla_\mu T^{\mu\nu} = 0$$

This is the covariant conservation of the energy-momentum tensor — the local expression of global coherence conservation in the curved coherence geometry.

### Step 3: The Curvature Side

The curvature of the coherence geometry is encoded in the **Einstein tensor**:

$$G_{\mu\nu} = R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2} R \, g_{\mu\nu}$$

where $R_{\mu\nu}$ is the Ricci curvature tensor and $R$ is the scalar curvature. The Einstein tensor satisfies:

$$\nabla_\mu G^{\mu\nu} = 0 \quad \text{(Bianchi identity)}$$

This is a geometric identity — it holds for any metric $g_{\mu\nu}$.

### Step 4: The Fixed-Point Equation

The self-consistency condition requires that the curvature be proportional to the coherence content that generates it:

$$\boxed{G_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} \, T_{\mu\nu}}$$

*Why this form?*

1. **Tensorial**: Both sides are symmetric rank-2 tensors — the appropriate geometric objects for relating curvature to source
2. **Conserved**: Both sides are covariantly divergence-free ($\nabla_\mu G^{\mu\nu} = 0$ by Bianchi; $\nabla_\mu T^{\mu\nu} = 0$ by Axiom 1)
3. **Unique at lowest order**: By Lovelock's theorem (1971), in four spacetime dimensions, the Einstein tensor is the unique divergence-free symmetric rank-2 tensor that is at most second-order in derivatives of the metric. Higher-order terms would introduce additional length scales not present in the minimal framework
4. **$G$ as coupling constant**: $8\pi G/c^4$ sets the strength of the curvature response to coherence content

### Step 5: Connection to the Bootstrap

The Einstein equations are the geometric manifestation of the [Bootstrap Mechanism](/derivations/interactions/bootstrap). The bootstrap generates a self-reinforcing hierarchy of observers and relational invariants. The Einstein equations express the same self-reinforcement at the level of geometry:

- Observers generate curvature (through their relational invariant density)
- Curvature determines observer trajectories (through the geodesic equation)
- Trajectories determine the distribution of observers (through the dynamics)
- The distribution determines the curvature (closing the loop)

The Einstein equations are the **unique** linear, covariantly conserved fixed-point conditions for this self-referential loop. They are not "laws of gravity" imposed externally — they are the self-consistency conditions of a universe populated by interacting observers.

### Step 6: The Cosmological Constant

The framework allows a cosmological constant $\Lambda$:

$$G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda \, g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} \, T_{\mu\nu}$$

In the framework, $\Lambda$ represents the coherence cost of the bare coherence geometry itself — the "vacuum energy" of the observer-free substrate. The $\Lambda g_{\mu\nu}$ term is the only additional term permitted by Lovelock's theorem that does not introduce higher derivatives.

## Rigor Assessment

**What is established:**
- The self-referential structure (geometry ↔ trajectories ↔ density ↔ geometry) is logically correct
- The conservation of $T_{\mu\nu}$ from Axiom 1 is a direct consequence
- Lovelock's theorem provides a rigorous uniqueness argument for the Einstein tensor in 4D
- The fixed-point interpretation is conceptually sound

**What needs work:**
- Lovelock's theorem requires 4D spacetime — the framework should derive 3+1 dimensions before invoking it (the [Three Dimensions](/derivations/dimensions/three-dimensions) derivation provides the spatial part)
- The coupling constant $G$ is introduced without derivation
- The actual construction of $T_{\mu\nu}$ from the observer network is not carried out
- Existence and uniqueness of the fixed point (for given initial data) requires PDE theory

## Open Gaps

1. **Cosmological constant problem**: The value of $\Lambda$ — the deepest open problem in theoretical physics — should in principle be computable from the coherence geometry of the substrate.
2. **Singularity resolution**: The Einstein equations predict singularities. The framework's discrete relational invariant network should resolve these at the Planck scale.
3. **Quantum gravity**: The full quantum treatment requires quantizing the coherence geometry itself, connecting to the holographic derivations.
