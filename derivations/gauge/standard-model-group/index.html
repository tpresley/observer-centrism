Step 3: The Product Structure

Proof. The three gauge factors arise from three distinct algebraic structures in the Cayley-Dickson hierarchy:

  2. SU(2)SU(2)SU(2) from H\mathbb{H}H: the quaternionic extension in 3D
  3. SU(3)SU(3)SU(3) from O/H\mathbb{O}/\mathbb{H}O/H: the octonionic complement of the quaternionic subalgebra

  • U(1)U(1)U(1) is the automorphism of C\mathbb{C}C (phase rotation)
  • SU(2)SU(2)SU(2) is the unit group of H\mathbb{H}H (quaternion multiplication)

No simple group G⊃GSMG \supset G_{SM}G⊃GSM​ arises because:

  • A simple group containing U(1)×SU(2)×SU(3)U(1) \times SU(2) \times SU(3)U(1)×SU(2)×SU(3) would require a single algebraic structure encompassing all three. But the three arise from different algebraic operations on the division algebra tower.
  • The smallest simple group containing GSMG_{SM}GSM​ is SU(5)SU(5)SU(5) (Georgi-Glashow, 1974). But SU(5)SU(5)SU(5) requires a 5-dimensional fundamental representation, which would correspond to a 10-dimensional normed division algebra — but no such algebra exists (Hurwitz). □\square□
