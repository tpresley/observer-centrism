Statement

Axiom 3 (Loop Closure). The internal dynamics of every observer are cyclic: the state returns to its initial configuration after a finite period. A loop that does not close eventually dissipates its invariant and dissolves. Loop closure is not an additional constraint — it is what makes an observer persistent in the sense of Axiom 2.

Structural Postulate

Postulate (Smooth metric structure). The state space Σ\SigmaΣ of an observer O=(Σ,I,B)\mathcal{O} = (\Sigma, I, \mathcal{B})O=(Σ,I,B) (Axiom 2) carries:

(S1) A smooth manifold structure (of finite dimension dim⁡Σ≥1\dim \Sigma \geq 1dimΣ≥1)

(S2) A Riemannian metric ggg on Σ\SigmaΣ that is GOG_\mathcal{O}GO​-invariant: ϕt∗g=g\phi_t^* g = gϕt∗​g=g for all ttt

Remark (Status of S1–S2). These are structural postulates — they specify the mathematical setting in which Axiom 3 operates, analogous to the topology postulate in Observer Definition. They are not derived from Axioms 1–2. The smooth structure is required for Noether’s theorem (Step 3), and the metric for Lyapunov stability (Step 4) and the coherence cost integral (Step 6). The GOG_\mathcal{O}GO​-invariance of ggg ensures that the dynamics preserves geometric quantities (distances, volumes) on Σ\SigmaΣ, consistent with Hamiltonian phase flow.
