Observer-Centrism

A Framework for Physics, Mind, and Structure

A derivation of quantum mechanics, general relativity, thermodynamics, the particle spectrum, holography, and the basis of experience from three axioms.

The minimal observer pair: two phase oscillators with opposite winding numbers (+1, −1), exchanging coherence while the total is conserved. Each loop closes on itself — the simplest structure satisfying all three axioms.

Axiom 1: Coherence Conservation

There exists a primitive quantity — coherence — that is globally conserved. Coherence cannot be created from nothing, destroyed, or exported to an external reservoir. The ontology is closed.

Axiom 2: The Observer Definition

Any structure that maintains at least one invariant quantity across transformations, together with a distinction between transformations that preserve that invariant (self) and those that threaten it (non-self), qualifies as an observer.

Axiom 3: Loop Closure

Observer dynamics must be closed loops. The internal dynamics of an observer must be cyclic: the state must return, at least approximately, to its initial configuration after a finite time.
