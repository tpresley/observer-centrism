## Prediction

The Standard Model gauge structure $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$ is constrained by the three-dimensional coherence geometry fixed point. No larger gauge group exists at higher energies. **Proton decay will not be observed.**

## Derivation Sketch

1. The gauge structure emerges from the coherence geometry's fixed-point solution
2. This solution is unique in $d = 3$ (no higher symmetry to break from)
3. Without a GUT group, there are no gauge bosons mediating proton decay
4. Baryon number conservation is exact, not approximate

## Current Evidence

Super-Kamiokande has set lower bounds exceeding $10^{34}$ years for dominant GUT decay modes ($p \to e^+ \pi^0$), already ruling out minimal $SU(5)$. Hyper-Kamiokande will push sensitivity further.
