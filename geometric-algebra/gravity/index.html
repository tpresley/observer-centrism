Target: Gravity as Coherence Geometry Curvature (status: rigorous)

The target derivation establishes that massive observers curve the coherence geometry, with geodesics as minimum coherence cost paths and the equivalence principle as structural. The proof has three layers: metric-density coupling (Theorem 0.1, from action duality + coherence subadditivity), inverse-square density falloff, and the Schwarzschild solution as the unique spherically symmetric vacuum geometry.

Gauge Theory Gravity (GTG), developed by Lasenby, Doran, and Gull (1998), recasts all of general relativity within flat Spacetime Algebra. Instead of a curved manifold with a metric gμνg_{\mu\nu}gμν​ and Christoffel connection Γνρμ\Gamma^\mu_{\nu\rho}Γνρμ​, GTG works on flat Cl⁡(1,3)\operatorname{Cl}(1,3)Cl(1,3) with two gauge fields: a position gauge h‾(a)\underline{h}(a)h​(a) encoding how physical distances deviate from flat-space distances, and a rotation gauge Ω(a)\Omega(a)Ω(a) encoding how frames rotate under parallel transport. The Riemann curvature becomes a bivector-valued function, geodesics become rotor equations, and the equivalence principle becomes a gauge symmetry statement. This reformulation provides algebraic transparency for the framework’s gravitational derivation without changing its physical content.

The curved manifold is an emergent description, not fundamental.   2. Natural horizon regularity. The Schwarzschild coordinate singularity at r=RSr = R_Sr=RS​ is an artifact of the standard metric formulation. In GTG, the gauge fields are smooth at the horizon, producing the Gullstrand-Painlevé form naturally. This provides cleaner mathematical objects for the framework’s singularity resolution and curvature bound arguments.

  3. Separation of metric and connection. GTG separates the metric content (h‾\underline{h}h​, how distances are stretched) from the connection content (Ω\OmegaΩ, how frames rotate). The framework’s metric-density coupling (Theorem 0.1) acts primarily on h‾\underline{h}h​ — it is the distance structure that responds to coherence content.   4. Gravitational waves on flat background. In GTG, gravitational waves propagate on the flat STA background. Not a genuine simplification:
