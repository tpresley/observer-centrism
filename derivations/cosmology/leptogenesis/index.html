Motivation

The Baryogenesis derivation establishes that the framework satisfies the Sakharov conditions via coherence-dual pair CP violation. However, the CKM CP phase alone is too small to generate sufficient asymmetry. The framework predicts Majorana neutrinos with potentially large PMNS CP phases, opening a more efficient leptogenesis pathway:

  1. Heavy right-handed neutrinos (from the seesaw mechanism in Neutrino Masses) decay out of equilibrium in the early universe
  2. CP violation in the decays generates a lepton asymmetry
  3. B+LB+LB+L-violating sphalerons convert the lepton asymmetry to a baryon asymmetry while preserving B−LB-LB−L

Derivation Strategy

  1. Majorana mass spectrum: From the Neutrino Masses seesaw mechanism, determine the heavy right-handed neutrino mass scale MRM_RMR​.

  2. CP asymmetry parameter: Compute the CP asymmetry εi\varepsilon_iεi​ in heavy neutrino decays Ni→ℓHN_i \to \ell HNi​→ℓH from the interference of tree and loop diagrams, using the PMNS phases from Mixing Angles.

  3. Washout and efficiency: Estimate the washout factor κ\kappaκ from inverse decays and scattering processes. The efficiency depends on m~1=(Yν†Yν)11v2/M1\tilde{m}_1 = (Y_\nu^\dagger Y_\nu)_{11} v^2 / M_1m~1​=(Yν†​Yν​)11​v2/M1​.

  4. Sphaleron conversion: Apply the sphaleron conversion factor 28/7928/7928/79 to obtain ηB\eta_BηB​ from ηL\eta_LηL​.

  5. Consistency check: Verify that the predicted ηB\eta_BηB​ is consistent with the CMB-measured value ηB=(6.14±0.02)×10−10\eta_B = (6.14 \pm 0.02) \times 10^{-10}ηB​=(6.14±0.02)×10−10.

Open Gaps

Gap 1. The heavy right-handed neutrino mass scale MRM_RMR​ is not precisely determined by the Neutrino Masses derivation. The leptogenesis asymmetry is sensitive to this scale — the Davidson-Ibarra bound requires M1≳109M_1 \gtrsim 10^9M1​≳109 GeV for sufficient asymmetry.

Gap 2. The PMNS CP phases are constrained by the Mixing Angles derivation but not numerically predicted. The Dirac phase δCP\delta_{CP}δCP​ and Majorana phases α21,α31\alpha_{21}, \alpha_{31}α21​,α31​ all contribute to leptogenesis.

Gap 3. Resonant leptogenesis (when two heavy neutrinos are nearly degenerate) could relax the mass-scale bound. Whether the framework’s seesaw mechanism produces the requisite near-degeneracy is unknown.
