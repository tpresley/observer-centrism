Prior Whitepaper: Idea Analysis

An earlier version of the Observer-Centrism framework (dated October 2025) used a different mathematical foundation — information geometry, KL divergence, and a specific variational action — to pursue many of the same goals. That version was less rigorous and likely contains logic and mathematical errors, but some of its ideas may be worth pursuing within the current, more rigorous framework.

This page catalogs every distinct idea from that whitepaper, assesses whether it is already covered by the current framework, and evaluates the viability of absorbing ideas that are not yet covered. Viability is judged by whether an idea is plausibly derivable from the current framework's axioms and derivation chain, regardless of the logic used in the whitepaper.

KL divergence as fundamental variational principle

Partial Medium
Core Postulate (§1)

Observers minimize the Kullback-Leibler divergence D(q||p_θ) between their predictive model and empirical encounters. Persistence requires informational equilibrium.

Coverage in current framework

The current framework's Axiom 1 (coherence conservation) serves a similar structural role — both enforce self-consistency — but uses a subadditive measure on a σ-algebra rather than KL divergence. The specific information-theoretic machinery (predictive models, empirical distributions) is absent.

Viability for absorption

The KL divergence could potentially be derived as a consequence of coherence conservation applied to statistical descriptions. The connection would run: coherence conservation → constraints on probability assignments → KL-like divergence as the unique measure of consistency. However, the current framework already achieves what KL minimization is intended to achieve (stable observers) through loop closure and coherence conservation, so this may be a parallel formulation rather than an extension.

Not covered Medium
Core Postulate (§1), Info Geometry (§2)

The connection would strengthen the action-planck derivation's structural postulate S1 (smooth coherence measure). Worth pursuing as a mathematical deepening, but not required for existing results.

Quaternionic structure → SU(2) → weak force / spin

Partial Medium
Higher-Order Closures (§5)

Maintaining coherence across three orthogonal inference channels requires quaternionic structure (I,J,K with I²=J²=K²=IJK=-1). Covariance under rotation of the triple introduces SU(2) connection, producing spinorial fields and the weak interaction.

Coverage in current framework

The current framework derives spin from Z₂ winding classes in 3D (spin-statistics derivation) and three generations from dim SO(3) = 3. The SU(2) weak gauge symmetry is not explicitly derived. The topological approach (winding classes) differs fundamentally from the algebraic approach (quaternions).

Viability for absorption

The quaternionic derivation of SU(2) is elegant but the connection to the current framework's topological approach needs work. The frameworks are not contradictory — quaternions are the double cover of SO(3), and Z₂ winding is the fundamental group of SO(3). The challenge is that the whitepaper's mechanism is algebraic (extending number systems) while the current framework's is topological (winding classes). A synthesis might be possible but would require careful development.
