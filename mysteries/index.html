Mysteries of Physics

Physics has accumulated a striking list of deep, unanswered questions — from why the universe exists at all, to why it has three spatial dimensions, to why quantum mechanics works the way it does. These mysteries span cosmology, particle physics, gravity, and the foundations of quantum theory. Most are treated as independent puzzles, each requiring its own dedicated research program.

Observer-centrism approaches them differently. Starting from three axioms about coherence, observers, and loop closure, the framework derives answers to these questions through a single connected chain of reasoning. If the framework survives empirical falsification — particularly through its quantitative predictions for holographic noise and dark matter structure — it would provide an unusually cohesive picture: not twenty-seven separate answers, but one interlocking structure where each resolution reinforces the others.

Below is each mystery, the framework’s proposed answer, and a link to the full derivation chain from axioms to conclusion.

A note on “resolved”: A mystery marked resolved means the framework produces a specific, traceable answer from its axioms — not that the question is settled in physics at large. These are derivations within the framework. Whether the framework itself is correct is an empirical question, addressed on the Predictions page.
