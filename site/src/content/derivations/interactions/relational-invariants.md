## Statement

The relational invariants generated by Type III interactions are the framework's most important structural feature. They are:

1. **Genuinely new conserved quantities** — real structures in the coherence geometry
2. **Irreducible** — cannot be decomposed into properties of either component
3. **Generative** — each new invariant creates new symmetries and degrees of freedom via the reverse Noether mechanism

## Derivation

### Construction of the Relational Invariant

Let $\mathcal{O}_1 = (\Sigma_1, I_1, G_1)$ and $\mathcal{O}_2 = (\Sigma_2, I_2, G_2)$ undergo a Type III interaction. By the definition of Type III, both $I_1$ and $I_2$ survive, and a new invariant $I_{12}$ appears.

**Definition.** The relational invariant $I_{12}$ is a function on the joint state space:

$$I_{12}: \Sigma_1 \times \Sigma_2 \to \mathbb{R}$$

satisfying:

1. **Conservation**: $I_{12}$ is invariant under the joint symmetry group $G_1 \times G_2$ acting on $\Sigma_1 \times \Sigma_2$
2. **Irreducibility**: There exist no functions $f: \Sigma_1 \to \mathbb{R}$ and $g: \Sigma_2 \to \mathbb{R}$ such that $I_{12}(\sigma_1, \sigma_2) = f(\sigma_1) + g(\sigma_2)$ for all $(\sigma_1, \sigma_2)$
3. **Non-triviality**: $I_{12}$ is not constant

### Why Irreducibility Is Possible

The coherence measure $\mathcal{C}$ is subadditive (from the [Coherence Conservation](/derivations/axioms/coherence-conservation) formalization):

$$\mathcal{C}(\mathcal{O}_1 \cup \mathcal{O}_2) \leq \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{O}_1) + \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{O}_2)$$

The deficit $\Delta\mathcal{C} = \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{O}_1) + \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{O}_2) - \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{O}_1 \cup \mathcal{O}_2) \geq 0$ is coherence that resides *in the relationship* rather than in either part. This deficit is the coherence content of the relational invariant:

$$\mathcal{C}(I_{12}) = \Delta\mathcal{C}$$

If the coherence measure were additive, $\Delta\mathcal{C} = 0$ and no relational invariant could form. Subadditivity is what makes Type III interactions possible.

### The Reverse Noether Mechanism

Noether's theorem (forward direction): every continuous symmetry corresponds to a conserved quantity.

**Reverse Noether** (the direction the framework uses): every new conserved quantity corresponds to a new continuous symmetry.

Given a new relational invariant $I_{12}$, the reverse Noether theorem guarantees:

**Step 1.** $I_{12}$ is a conserved charge. By the converse of Noether's theorem, there exists a one-parameter group of transformations $\{U_{12}(t)\}_{t \in \mathbb{R}}$ under which $I_{12}$ is the conserved quantity:

$$\frac{d}{dt} I_{12}(U_{12}(t) \cdot (\sigma_1, \sigma_2)) = 0$$

**Step 2.** This one-parameter group $\{U_{12}(t)\}$ constitutes a new symmetry — a transformation that was not admissible before the relational invariant existed. Before the Type III interaction, the joint symmetry group was $G_1 \times G_2$. After, it is enlarged to $G_1 \times G_2 \times U_{12}(1)$.

**Step 3.** New symmetries license new transformations. New admissible transformations are new **degrees of freedom** — directions in state space that the system can now explore.

$$\dim(\text{DOF after}) = \dim(\text{DOF before}) + \dim(\text{new symmetry generators})$$

### The Mechanism in Summary

$$\boxed{\text{Type III interaction} \to \text{new invariant } I_{12} \xrightarrow{\text{reverse Noether}} \text{new symmetry } U_{12} \to \text{new DOF}}$$

Each interaction not only conserves existing structure but **expands the space of possible structure**.

### Physical Instances

**Quantum entanglement.** When two quantum systems interact and become entangled, the entanglement is a relational invariant: a conserved correlation structure ($I_{12}$) that cannot be attributed to either system individually. The entanglement entropy $S = -\text{Tr}(\rho_A \log \rho_A)$ measures the coherence content of this relational invariant.

**Chemical bonds.** A covalent bond between two atoms is a relational invariant: a shared electron configuration irreducible to either atom alone. The bond energy is $\mathcal{C}(I_{12})$. The new DOF are vibrational and rotational modes of the molecule — modes that did not exist for isolated atoms.

**Causal relationships.** The causal relationship between two spacetime events is a relational invariant in the coherence dependency graph. Once established, it is conserved — causation cannot be undone.

### Conservation of Relational Invariants

Once generated, a relational invariant is conserved by Axiom 1. It can be:

- **Transferred**: to other observers via further Type I interactions
- **Absorbed**: into a composite via Type II fusion
- **Compounded**: into higher-order relational invariants via further Type III interactions

But it cannot be **destroyed**. This irreversibility is the structural basis of the arrow of time (see [Time as Phase Ordering](/derivations/thermodynamics/time)).

## Rigor Assessment

**What is established:**
- The construction of $I_{12}$ on the joint state space is well-defined
- Subadditivity of $\mathcal{C}$ provides the coherence budget for relational invariants
- The forward Noether theorem is standard; the converse holds locally

**What needs work:**
- The reverse Noether step assumes the converse holds globally. The global converse requires regularity conditions on $I_{12}$
- The symmetry group enlargement assumes the new symmetry commutes with the old ones. Non-abelian cases need treatment
- The DOF counting needs connection to physical observable degrees of freedom

## Open Gaps

1. **Entanglement mapping**: The identification of quantum entanglement with relational invariants needs a precise mapping between $\mathcal{C}$ and entanglement entropy.
2. **Generation rate**: How many relational invariants does a given Type III interaction produce? Likely depends on the dimensionality of the joint state space.
3. **Higher-order structure**: When $I_{12}$ interacts with $\mathcal{O}_3$, the resulting second-order relational invariant $I_{(12)3}$ forms an algebraic tower that needs formalization.
