2. The Axioms

Source Document

The Axioms

Observer-Centrism rests on three primitive commitments. Everything else — time, space, quantum mechanics, gravity, the particle spectrum — is derived structure. The axioms are stated here precisely, followed by a note on what is deliberately not postulated.

2.1 Axiom One: Coherence Conservation

There exists a primitive quantity — coherence — that is globally conserved. Coherence cannot be created from nothing, destroyed, or exported to an external reservoir. The ontology is closed: there is no bath, no sink, no outside into which coherence can flow.

Coherence is not defined in terms of anything more fundamental. It is the ontic primitive — the substrate from which everything else is constructed. Its conservation is the single constraint that drives all subsequent structure.

The name is chosen deliberately. Coherence in physics typically refers to stable phase relationships between oscillating systems — the property that makes interference possible, that distinguishes quantum superposition from classical mixture, that characterizes organized as opposed to disordered structure. The framework’s primitive generalizes this intuition to its most abstract form: coherence is whatever it is that organized, persistent, self-maintaining structures conserve.

2.2 Axiom Two: The Observer Definition

Any structure that maintains at least one invariant quantity across transformations, together with a distinction between transformations that preserve that invariant (self) and those that threaten it (non-self), qualifies as an observer.

This is a functional definition. It does not specify what observers are made of, only what they do. A spinning vortex in a fluid, a chemical oscillator, a particle with conserved charge, a living cell, a measuring apparatus, a human scientist — all qualify as observers if they satisfy the criterion. The definition is deliberately broad, because the framework’s claim is that observer-like structures are ubiquitous and necessary, not rare and special.

The self/non-self distinction is essential. Without it, maintaining an invariant is trivially easy — a system in complete isolation trivially preserves any property. The distinction requires that the observer exist in an environment capable of threatening the invariant, and that the observer have some structural sensitivity to that threat. This is what makes an observer a genuine dynamical entity rather than an inert object.

2.3 Axiom Three: Loop Closure

Observer dynamics must be closed loops. The internal dynamics of an observer must be cyclic: the state must return, at least approximately, to its initial configuration after a finite time. This is the stability condition — the requirement that distinguishes persistent observers from transient fluctuations.

A loop that does not close eventually dissipates. Its invariant fails. The observer dissolves back into the substrate. The loop closure condition is therefore not an additional constraint imposed on observers — it is what makes them observers in a lasting sense. Only closed loops maintain their invariants indefinitely.

2.4 What Is Not Postulated

Equally important is what the framework does not take as primitive. Space and time are not postulated — they are derived from the structure of observer interactions. The laws of quantum mechanics are not postulated — they emerge from the coherence conservation requirement. The existence of particles, fields, and forces is not assumed — particles are stable loop solutions, fields are their coherence geometry, forces are the effects of that geometry on other observers. The number of spatial dimensions is not assumed — it is derived from constraints on observer boundary stability, as developed in Section 8.

The framework also does not postulate observers as a special class of physical entity that exists alongside or above the physical world. Observers are physical structures satisfying a structural criterion. Their special status in the framework reflects their structural necessity — not a philosophical preference for mind over matter, but the derivable fact that observer-like structures must exist and are the units from which all physical structure is built.
