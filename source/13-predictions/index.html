13. Predictions That Differ from Current Theory

Predictions That Differ from Current Theory

A framework that only recovers existing results is philosophy; one that makes distinct, testable predictions is physics. Observer-Centrism makes several predictions that differ from the standard expectations of beyond-Standard-Model physics. Several are already consistent with current experimental results; others await future tests.

13.1 No Fourth Fermion Generation

The three fermion generations correspond to the three independent winding directions in three spatial dimensions — one winding per dimension. A fourth generation would require a fourth independent winding direction, which does not exist in d=3d = 3d=3. The framework predicts exactly three fermion generations as a topological necessity.

Current experimental evidence is consistent with this prediction. Precision measurements of the Z boson decay width at LEP constrain the number of light neutrino species to three, ruling out a fourth generation with a light neutrino. The framework provides a structural reason for this, rather than merely an empirical observation.

13.2 Proton Stability — No Grand Unification

Standard Grand Unified Theories (GUTs) embed the Standard Model gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1)SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) into a larger group (SU(5)SU(5)SU(5), SO(10)SO(10)SO(10), E8E_8E8​) that breaks at some high energy scale, predicting proton decay. The framework predicts that no such larger gauge group exists: the Standard Model gauge structure is the direct output of the three-dimensional coherence geometry fixed point, with no higher symmetry waiting to be broken. Proton decay will not be observed.

Current experimental lower bounds on the proton lifetime exceed 103410^{34}1034 years for the dominant GUT decay modes, already ruling out minimal SU(5)SU(5)SU(5). The framework predicts this bound continues to be respected indefinitely.

13.3 No Supersymmetric Partners

Supersymmetry requires a continuous symmetry between bosons and fermions — a transformation that rotates integer-winding loops into half-integer-winding loops. Topological winding classes are discrete, not continuous. There is no continuous symmetry between them; the gap between winding classes cannot be bridged smoothly. Superpartners do not exist.

The LHC has found no evidence of supersymmetric partners despite extensive searches to TeV energy scales. The framework predicts this result continues at all accessible energies — not because superpartners are too heavy to produce, but because the topological structure of three-dimensional coherence geometry does not permit them.

13.4 Exact Unitarity at All Scales

Unitary evolution — the preservation of total quantum mechanical probability — is, in the framework, the exact statement of coherence conservation applied to quantum dynamics. Coherence conservation is absolute: it admits no corrections, no exceptions, no scale at which it fails. The framework therefore predicts exact unitarity at all energy scales, in all systems, without exception. No objective collapse, no nonlinear corrections to the Schrodinger equation, no violation of unitarity in black hole evaporation.

Precision tests of quantum superposition with increasingly massive objects — matter-wave interferometry with molecules, nanoscale mechanical oscillators, and in future experiments with macroscopic objects — probe this prediction directly. The framework predicts no deviation from exact unitary evolution will be found.

13.5 The Great Desert

The framework predicts no new physics between the electroweak scale (246 GeV) and the Planck scale (101910^{19}1019 GeV). There is no GUT scale, no intermediate symmetry breaking, no new particles in the ‘desert’ between TeV and 101610^{16}1016 GeV. The hierarchy of crystallization scales is complete as observed: electroweak, QCD confinement, atomic, molecular — with no additional levels.

This is a strong prediction falsifiable by future colliders. If a new particle or force is discovered at an intermediate scale with no connection to the Standard Model’s symmetry structure, the framework requires revision. The great desert is a genuine prediction, not a post-hoc accommodation.

13.6 Holographic Noise with Causal Structure

The discrete relational invariant network underlying the continuum spacetime introduces irreducible position uncertainty at the Planck scale — holographic noise. This noise should have a specific correlation structure: correlated along null (light-cone) directions more than along spacelike directions, because the dependency graph is ordered by causal relationships.

This anisotropic correlation structure distinguishes the framework’s prediction from simpler models of Planck-scale discreteness. Next-generation space-based interferometers may approach the sensitivity required to detect or constrain this noise structure along null baselines.

13.7 Dark Matter Granularity

If dark matter consists of stable observer loops — as the framework requires of any persistent structure — it must have a characteristic coherence domain size set by its loop closure scale. This implies a minimum granularity: a minimum dark matter halo mass below which dark matter structures do not form, set by quantum pressure from the loop closure condition.

This prediction is consistent with observed suppressions of small-scale structure in dwarf galaxy populations and the matter power spectrum — the ‘missing satellites’ problem and related anomalies. The framework predicts these are structural signatures of the dark matter’s loop closure scale, not numerical artifacts of simulation resolution.
