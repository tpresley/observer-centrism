The Riemann tensor $\mathcal{R}(B)$ maps bivectors to bivectors -- it is a linear function on the 6-dimensional space of grade-2 elements. The Einstein equation $G(a) = \kappa\,T(a)$ operates on vectors, not index-laden tensors. Lovelock's uniqueness theorem (existing Theorem 3.2 in the derivation) may gain a cleaner GA proof. The framework's derivation uses Lovelock uniqueness and coherence conservation; GA provides an alternative index-free formulation.

## Connection to Framework Derivation

The target derivation [Einstein Field Equations as Fixed-Point Conditions](/derivations/spacetime/einstein-equations) has status **rigorous**. It proves that the Einstein field equations $G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda\,g_{\mu\nu} = (8\pi G/c^4)\,T_{\mu\nu}$ are the unique self-consistency (fixed-point) conditions of the coherence geometry, using Lovelock's uniqueness theorem in $3+1$ dimensions. The conservation law $\nabla_\mu T^{\mu\nu} = 0$ is the local expression of coherence conservation in curved spacetime.

The GA exploration asks whether the index-free STA formulation simplifies the uniqueness argument and makes the fixed-point structure more transparent.

## GA Structure

Primary algebra: $\operatorname{Cl}(1,3)$ (Spacetime Algebra).

Key GA objects to develop:

1. **Riemann bivector map** $\mathcal{R}(B)$ — a linear function $\bigwedge^2 \to \bigwedge^2$ encoding all curvature information; decomposes into Weyl, Ricci, and scalar parts via grade projections
2. **Ricci extraction** $\mathcal{R}(a) = \partial_b \cdot \mathcal{R}(b \wedge a)$ — the Ricci tensor as a vector-valued function of vectors, obtained by contracting one slot of the Riemann map
3. **Einstein function** $G(a) = \mathcal{R}(a) - \frac{1}{2}\,a\,R$ — the index-free Einstein tensor acting on a vector $a$, with $R = \partial_a \cdot \mathcal{R}(a)$ the Ricci scalar
4. **Field equation** $G(a) + \Lambda\,a = \kappa\,T(a)$ — a single vector equation replacing the $4 \times 4$ symmetric tensor equation, with automatic conservation $\partial_a \cdot [G(a) + \Lambda\,a] = 0$

## Open Questions

- Does the STA formulation of Lovelock's theorem (uniqueness of $G(a)$ among divergence-free symmetric vector functions of curvature) yield a shorter or more illuminating proof than the standard tensor index proof?
- Can the fixed-point interpretation (curvature generated by observers must be consistent with their trajectories) be stated as a GA self-referential equation on the gauge fields $\underline{h}$ and $\Omega$?
- Does the decomposition of $\mathcal{R}(B)$ into irreducible grade components provide geometric insight into which parts of curvature are sourced by coherence (Ricci) versus propagate freely (Weyl)?
